Abstract

The “leaky thalamus” hypothesis posits that schizophrenia arises primarily from impaired sensory gating in the thalamic reticular nucleus (TRN), driven by weak GABA braking (low k) and metabolic glucose dysregulation. Using a dynamical-systems model, we ran a 10,000-patient Monte Carlo simulation comparing standard care to an innovative Nature-First Hybrid Smart Patch: closed-loop low-intensity focused ultrasound (LIFU), ketogenic metabolic therapy, and high-purity broad-spectrum hemp cannabinoid extract (CBX).

The natural CBX-enhanced arm achieved an 85.5% mean symptom reduction, 99.1% strong responders, superior cognitive restoration, and the lowest side-effect burden. We speculate on a practical daily regimen with estimated 2026 costs and call for immediate Phase 0 human pilots.
