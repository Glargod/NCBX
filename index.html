Abstract

This thesis models schizophrenia as impaired sensory filtering in the thalamic reticular nucleus (TRN). Using dynamical systems mathematics, we simulated 10,000 virtual patients comparing standard care to a Nature-First Hybrid Smart Patch: closed-loop LIFU neuromodulation, ketogenic metabolic therapy, and high-purity broad-spectrum hemp cannabinoid extract (CBX).

The natural CBX-enhanced arm showed the strongest simulated outcomes. This work is purely computational and speculative — intended to inspire rigorous future research.

The Leaky Thalamus Theory

Core dynamical model:

dx/dt = −k·x + η(t) + εglucose

Where k = TRN filter strength (GABA braking), η(t) = sensory noise, and εglucose = metabolic inflammation.
