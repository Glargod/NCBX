Abstract

This thesis models schizophrenia symptomatology through impaired sensory gating in the thalamic reticular nucleus (TRN), deliberately omitting dopaminergic dynamics to isolate thalamocortical filtering, metabolic support, and inflammatory load.

Using coupled differential equations and Monte Carlo simulation across heterogeneous virtual patients, we compare baseline gating failure against a Nature-First Hybrid Intervention: closed-loop LIFU neuromodulation, ketogenic metabolic therapy (Keto 2.0), and high-purity broad-spectrum hemp cannabinoid extract (CBX).

The full package produces robust simulated gating recovery and symptom reduction under realistic biological variability. Purely computational and speculative — designed to inspire targeted future research.

dx/dt = −αₓ x + βₓ (1−g)(s₀ + η(t))
